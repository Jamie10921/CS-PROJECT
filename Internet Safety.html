What is INTERNET SAFETY?

Internet or Online Safety is securing the user's personal information and avoiding security risks and the self-protection from computer crime in general. Common concerns and dangers on the internet include hostile users, websites and software and various types of obscene or offensive content such as pornography and samples of terrorist or criminal activity. Several crimes can be committed on the Internet such as stalking, identity theft and more. Numerous groups, and governments and organizations have expressed concerns and are dedicated to the safety of children using the Internet such as the National Online Safety Campaign, Cybersafe Asia, and SaferKidsPH.

Did you know?

Safer Internet Day is celebrated worldwide on February 11 to raise awareness about internet safety and current online issues.

The DANGERS of the Internet

  1. Identity Theft

    This is the unauthorized use of someone's personal information (such as name, social security number, or credit card information) for personal use. One with the proper skills could easily impersonate you to hack into your bank account or use your credit card and take your money.

  2. Cyberbullying

    School-age children typically experience this online bullying over social media platforms. For more on this, visit our page on Cyberbullying.

  3. Cyberpredators

    Many young children nowadays fall victim to online sexual predators. These predators are typically adults masquerading as friendly strangers in order to gain the child's trust and easily manipulate and harm them. This is also knows as Online Grooming.

  4. Internet Fraud

    This is a kind of crime that involves misleading or deceiving a "victim" in the hopes to steal their money/property. This is more commonly known as scamming. Usually, the scammer contacts you (through email, text, or call) and tries to obtain important information such as your back account or your social security number to steal your money.

Why is online safety important?

TIPS:

  • Keep strong passwords
  • Secure your privacy settings on all forms of media.
  • Do not share valuable personal information, especially to strangers and to unsecure sites
  • Practice safe browsing
  • Don't talk to strangers, make sure you know and trust whoever you're talking to online
  • Do not open and reply to emails from people you don't know
  • Check if you can trust the source of the download and if others have experience in downloading it. This is so that viruses won't get into your device.
  • Purchase items only from secure and trusted sites
  • Install anti-virus programs
